# Distractor Cards

Print-ready set. Cut along the rules. Each card has a title, a 60–90 word "breaking news" narrative, and two or three facilitator prompts to read at the table.

The intent of a distractor is not to make the team restart their roadmap. It is to make them **visibly adapt** it — move items, change colours, add a dependency, surface a stakeholder conversation they would otherwise have ducked.

## Distribution rules

- **Every team** receives **both general distractors** in Round 1.
- **Each team** also receives **one unique per-team distractor** in Round 1.
- **Round 2 (10 minutes before the end of build time)**: every team receives the **same final distractor**, chosen by the facilitator from the per-team set for maximum impact (the new CEO with B2C focus is the default).

---

## General distractors — every team gets these

### G1 · International tariffs

**Breaking news.** The government of Victory's largest export market has just announced new import tariffs of 18% on finished apparel, effective the start of next quarter. Victory's COO is on the phone to the CTO: half the supply contracts assume the old rates. Procurement want to re-route through Turkey; Finance wants to re-price. The board meets on Friday.

**At your table:**

- What moves on your roadmap, and what gets cut?
- Which technical capability suddenly becomes urgent that was not before?
- Which stakeholder do you call first, and what do you tell them?

---

### G2 · Data breach from the customer portal

**Breaking news.** The supplier-facing Custom Portal has been breached overnight. The blast radius is unclear; the security team is still triaging, but design drafts for two upcoming seasons may be in the wild. PR will field questions by lunchtime. Legal is on the line about GDPR notification windows. The CISO wants the portal off the public internet by end of day.

**At your table:**

- Does anything on your roadmap accelerate?
- Does anything get paused or replaced?
- Where does the trust pillar of Well-Architected show up on your board now — and was it visible before?

## Per-team distractors — one per team

### T1 · New technology in the market

**Breaking news.** A French startup, *Heuritech*, has just released a fashion-trend prediction engine widely covered by the trade press, with claimed 70% accuracy on six-month seasonal trend calls. Two competitors have signed up in the last fortnight. The CDO has read the same article you have and is "very keen to understand our position".

**At your table:**

- Does this go on the roadmap? Where, and at what cost to what else?
- How do you defend the answer "not yet" if that is the right answer?
- Which of your existing capabilities, if built well, would make this easy to add later?

---

### T2 · New wholesale channel

**Breaking news.** A regional buying group representing 1,800 independent boutiques has approached Victory with a wholesale partnership. The deal is real, the numbers are real, and the CFO is already modelling them. The catch: they want EDI integration, real-time stock, and B2B portal access by the start of next quarter.

**At your table:**

- Does any of your planned B2C work serve this, or are you suddenly running two programmes?
- What gets re-sequenced to free up integration capacity?
- What do you say to the CFO about the timeline?

---

### T3 · New CEO with B2C focus

**Breaking news.** The board has appointed a new CEO with a strong direct-to-consumer pedigree from a digitally native brand. Her first all-hands is next Monday. The CTO has been asked to "walk her through the roadmap on Wednesday and explain how we are accelerating the consumer story". The B2B sponsors are quietly nervous.

**At your table:**

- Which items on your roadmap suddenly look under-invested? Which look over-invested?
- How do you tell the B2B story without sounding defensive?
- Does the audience for your roadmap change — and therefore does the *type* of roadmap need to change?

---

### T4 · Regulatory change: the EU AI Act

**Breaking news.** The legal team has flagged that several of your AI ambitions — the personal-shopper agent, the AI-assisted design tool, the seasonal demand forecast — are likely to be classified as "limited" or "high" risk under the EU AI Act, with documentation, transparency, and human-oversight obligations attached. Compliance want a written plan within a month.

**At your table:**

- Which AI items stay, which pause, and which need a compliance workstream attached?
- Does a new swimlane appear on your roadmap?
- Where does *Trusted* show up now, and how do you make it visible to the board?

---

### T5 · ERP upgrade (EMEA only)

**Breaking news.** The EMEA regional ERP team have announced an upgrade to the next major version, mandatory within the next two quarters. They expect a freeze on new integrations during the upgrade window, and a behaviour change in the inventory API afterwards. The other regions are not affected.

**At your table:**

- Which of your inventory or supply-chain items hit the freeze window?
- Do you accelerate, delay, or split by region?
- What does this do to the "single view" promise you made to the board?

---

### T6 · New luxury line of business

**Breaking news.** Leadership have approved the launch of a new premium sub-brand, *Victory Atelier*, targeting a luxury audience with lower volumes, higher margins, and a completely different customer experience: appointment-only, made-to-measure, hand-finished. The first capsule drops in nine months. The CDO wants to know whether the existing roadmap "covers it".

**At your table:**

- Does Atelier share capabilities with core Victory, or does it need its own swimlane?
- Which existing items become genuinely reusable, and which were single-purpose?
- How do you communicate the answer without losing executive confidence in either business?

---

### T7 · Key supplier breaches anti-slavery law

**Breaking news.** Investigative journalists have published a story tying one of Victory's three largest manufacturing partners to forced labour. The board's position is immediate: the relationship ends today. Procurement need to redistribute the volume; PR are preparing a statement; the new ESG officer wants every supplier on the books re-audited.

**At your table:**

- Which capability on your roadmap is suddenly the most important one, and was it visible before?
- What does this do to the supplier-portal and SRM items?
- How do you embed *governance* into the roadmap without making it a separate, ignored swimlane?

---

### T8 · Cash-flow constraint on a key product line

**Breaking news.** An unexpectedly bad season in outerwear (climate, not demand) has put pressure on cash. The CFO has told the CTO that capex for the next two quarters is cut by 30% across the board, with the option to protect "one revenue-generating capability" on the roadmap.

**At your table:**

- Which one do you protect, and how do you defend the choice?
- What gets paused, and what gets killed outright?
- How do you preserve sequencing so the cut items can come back without restarting?

---

### T9 · File storage reaches capacity

**Breaking news.** The platform team have raised a P1: shared file storage holding design drafts, supplier documents, and historical commerce assets is at 94% and growing. Procurement of additional capacity takes a quarter. Migration to cloud object storage has been "planned" for two years.

**At your table:**

- Does this technical-debt item make it onto the board, or do you let it stay invisible?
- Which business capability does it actually unlock?
- How do you talk about technical debt to a board that does not want to hear about it?

---

### T10 · Design tool vendor acquired

**Breaking news.** The 3rd-party design tool you depend on has been acquired by a large platform vendor, which has announced it will expose design assets externally to "influencers and creators" via a new marketplace in six months. Your designers are nervous. Marketing are intrigued. Legal are reading the new terms now.

**At your table:**

- Does this become a risk item, an opportunity item, or both, on your roadmap?
- What does it do to the AI-assisted design tool capability?
- Do you build a replacement now, or do you exploit the change?

---

## Final distractor (Round 2) — choose one for everybody

The default final distractor is **T3 · New CEO with B2C focus**, dropped on every team 10 minutes before the end of build time. It is the highest-impact card in the set because it forces teams to ask whether the *audience* for the roadmap has changed — which is the real test of whether they understood the theory in Block 1.

Pick a different card if your room is leaning a particular way: T4 for an AI-heavy audience, T7 for a sustainability-focused one, T8 for an audience that has been ignoring constraints.

The teaching point is the same regardless: **a good roadmap absorbs change without collapsing.** Teams that have built capability-first roadmaps will adapt cleanly. Teams that have built thinly disguised project plans will start over. Both lessons are valuable; let the difference speak.
